# Investment Scam

## Overview

An investment scam is a type of fraud in which scammers deceive individuals into investing money in fake, misleading or non-existent investment opportunities. These scams often promise high returns with little or no risk, claiming that the investment is exclusive, time-sensitive or guaranteed to generate substantial profits. Scammers may impersonate financial advisors, investment firms, trading platforms or experienced investors to gain credibility. Investment scams commonly involve cryptocurrencies, foreign exchange (Forex), stocks, commodities, precious metals and other financial products. The primary objective is to convince victims to transfer money, after which the scammers either disappear or continue requesting additional payments under various pretexts.

## Common Scam Tactics

- Promising guaranteed or exceptionally high investment returns with minimal risk.
- Claiming that the investment opportunity is exclusive or available for a limited time.
- Impersonating licensed investment professionals or financial institutions.
- Creating fake investment platforms that display fabricated profits.
- Encouraging victims to start with a small investment before requesting larger amounts.
- Using fake testimonials, success stories or celebrity endorsements to build credibility.
- Contacting victims through social media, messaging applications or unsolicited phone calls.
- Manipulating victims with fabricated account balances showing unrealistic profits.
- Requesting additional payments for taxes, withdrawal fees or account verification before allowing withdrawals.
- Pressuring victims to recruit friends or family members to increase their investments.

## Common Warning Signs

- Guarantees of high returns with little or no investment risk.
- Pressure to invest immediately to avoid missing an opportunity.
- Unsolicited investment recommendations from strangers.
- Investment platforms that lack clear company information or regulatory licensing.
- Difficulty withdrawing funds despite showing profits.
- Requests for additional payments before profits can be withdrawn.
- Communication primarily conducted through private messaging applications.
- Unrealistic claims of consistent or rapid financial growth.
- Vague explanations of how the investment generates returns.
- Requests to keep the investment opportunity confidential.

## Typical Victim Requests

- Transfer money to investment or trading accounts.
- Create accounts on unfamiliar investment platforms.
- Purchase cryptocurrencies to fund investments.
- Provide personal identification documents for account verification.
- Pay withdrawal fees, taxes or administrative charges before accessing profits.
- Increase investment amounts after initial returns appear successful.
- Invite additional investors to participate in the opportunity.
- Share banking information or payment details.

## Prevention Tips

- Research investment opportunities thoroughly before committing any funds.
- Verify that investment companies and representatives are properly licensed where applicable.
- Be cautious of promises of guaranteed or unusually high returns.
- Avoid making investment decisions under pressure or artificial deadlines.
- Use only reputable investment platforms and financial institutions.
- Seek independent financial advice before making significant investments.
- Be sceptical of investment opportunities introduced by strangers online.
- Verify company information through official sources before transferring money.
- Remember that all legitimate investments carry some level of risk.

## Recommended Actions

- Stop communicating with the suspected scammer immediately.
- Do not transfer additional funds, even if promised that existing investments can be recovered.
- Contact your bank immediately if money has already been transferred.
- Preserve screenshots, transaction records, emails and chat conversations as evidence.
- Report suspicious investment platforms or individuals to the appropriate authorities.
- File a police report if financial loss has occurred.
- Monitor financial accounts for unauthorised transactions.
- Warn family members and friends if they may also have been approached by the same scam.

## Singapore Context

Investment scams have become increasingly common in Singapore, particularly those involving cryptocurrency investments, online trading platforms and fraudulent wealth-building opportunities promoted through social media and messaging applications. Scammers often establish long-term communication with victims before introducing investment opportunities and may use fake trading platforms that display fabricated profits to encourage larger investments.

The Singapore Police Force (SPF) regularly publishes advisories on emerging investment scam trends and encourages the public to verify investment opportunities before transferring funds. The Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) reminds consumers to deal only with appropriately regulated financial institutions and to be cautious of investment offers that promise guaranteed returns. ScamShield helps reduce exposure to scam calls and messages, while the Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA) promotes cybersecurity awareness to reduce online fraud.

## Additional Notes

Investment scams commonly exploit greed, trust and fear of missing out (FOMO). Scammers often spend considerable time building relationships with victims before introducing investment opportunities, making the scams appear more credible. In many cases, victims initially receive small returns or are shown fabricated profits to encourage larger investments.

Legitimate investments involve varying levels of financial risk, and no investment can guarantee consistently high returns without risk. Users should evaluate investment opportunities carefully, verify regulatory information independently and avoid making financial decisions based solely on persuasive marketing, social media recommendations or pressure from unfamiliar individuals. If an investment opportunity appears unusually profitable with little explanation of its risks, it should be treated with caution.
