# Romance Scam

## Overview

A romance scam is a type of fraud in which scammers establish fake romantic or emotional relationships with victims to gain their trust before exploiting them for financial gain or personal information. These scams commonly begin through social media platforms, dating applications, messaging services or online communities. Scammers often spend weeks or even months building a close relationship with the victim by expressing affection, providing emotional support and creating a sense of commitment. Once trust has been established, they fabricate emergencies, financial difficulties or future plans together to persuade victims to send money or disclose sensitive information.

## Common Scam Tactics

- Creating fake online profiles using stolen photographs and fabricated personal information.
- Initiating conversations through dating applications, social media platforms or messaging services.
- Building emotional relationships by communicating frequently and expressing affection quickly.
- Claiming to work overseas, serve in the military or travel frequently to avoid meeting in person.
- Inventing emergencies involving medical expenses, travel costs or financial hardships.
- Promising future meetings, marriage or relocation together.
- Requesting financial assistance through bank transfers, gift cards or cryptocurrency.
- Avoiding video calls or making excuses for repeatedly cancelling planned meetings.
- Sending fake documents such as passports, travel itineraries or medical reports to support fabricated stories.
- Continuing to create new emergencies that require additional financial assistance.

## Common Warning Signs

- Rapid declarations of love or emotional commitment despite knowing each other for only a short time.
- Refusal or inability to meet in person or participate in live video calls.
- Frequent requests for money, gifts or financial assistance.
- Stories involving repeated emergencies, accidents or unexpected financial problems.
- Inconsistent personal details or changing life stories.
- Requests to keep the relationship or financial assistance private.
- Pressure to make urgent financial decisions.
- Communication conducted exclusively through messaging applications or social media.
- Requests for personal identification documents or banking information.
- Profiles with limited online history or suspiciously perfect photographs.

## Typical Victim Requests

- Transfer money for medical treatment, travel expenses or emergency situations.
- Purchase gift cards or digital vouchers.
- Send cryptocurrency or international money transfers.
- Provide bank account details or personal identification documents.
- Receive and transfer money on behalf of the scammer.
- Invest in financial opportunities introduced by the scammer.
- Share confidential personal information.
- Continue providing financial support for newly created emergencies.

## Prevention Tips

- Take time to develop online relationships and avoid making decisions based solely on emotions.
- Be cautious of individuals who express strong romantic feelings unusually quickly.
- Verify the person's identity through live video calls or other reliable methods.
- Never send money to someone you have not met and independently verified.
- Be sceptical of repeated requests for financial assistance.
- Discuss online relationships with trusted family members or friends before making financial commitments.
- Protect personal information and avoid sharing sensitive documents.
- Research profile photographs and information if authenticity is uncertain.
- Be cautious of excuses that repeatedly prevent face-to-face meetings.

## Recommended Actions

- Stop communicating with the suspected scammer immediately.
- Do not send additional money or provide further personal information.
- Block the individual's accounts on social media and messaging platforms.
- Contact your bank immediately if money has already been transferred.
- Preserve chat conversations, payment records and profile information as evidence.
- Report the fraudulent account to the relevant social media or dating platform.
- File a police report if financial loss has occurred.
- Inform trusted family members or friends for emotional and practical support.
- Monitor financial accounts and online accounts for suspicious activity.

## Singapore Context

Romance scams continue to affect individuals in Singapore and frequently begin through dating applications, social media platforms and messaging services. Scammers often invest significant time building emotional relationships before requesting financial assistance for fabricated emergencies or introducing victims to fraudulent investment opportunities. In some cases, romance scams evolve into investment scams after trust has been established.

The Singapore Police Force (SPF) regularly publishes advisories highlighting romance scam trends and encourages members of the public to remain cautious when developing online relationships. ScamShield helps reduce exposure to scam calls and messages, while the Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA) promotes cybersecurity awareness and safe online practices. Members of the public are encouraged to verify online identities independently and avoid sending money to individuals they have never met in person.

## Additional Notes

Romance scams rely primarily on emotional manipulation rather than technical deception. Scammers carefully build trust over an extended period, making victims feel emotionally connected before introducing financial requests. Because victims often believe they are helping someone they care about, these scams can continue for months and may result in substantial financial losses.

A genuine relationship should not depend on repeated financial assistance or constant emergency payments. Users should remain cautious if an online acquaintance consistently avoids meeting in person, requests secrecy or introduces urgent financial problems shortly after establishing a relationship. Independent verification, open discussions with trusted family members or friends and taking time before making financial decisions are among the most effective ways to reduce the risk of becoming a victim of a romance scam.
