# Parcel Scam

## Overview

A parcel scam is a type of fraud in which scammers exploit online shopping, courier services and parcel deliveries to deceive individuals into making payments, revealing personal information or clicking malicious links. These scams often involve fake delivery notifications, claims that a parcel cannot be delivered, or messages stating that customs duties, taxes or redelivery fees must be paid before a package can be released. In some cases, scammers impersonate courier companies or postal services to create a sense of legitimacy. Parcel scams have become increasingly common as online shopping and home deliveries continue to grow, making fraudulent delivery notifications appear more believable.

## Common Scam Tactics

- Impersonating courier companies, postal services or delivery personnel.
- Sending SMS messages or emails claiming that a parcel cannot be delivered due to an incorrect address.
- Requesting payment of customs duties, shipping charges or redelivery fees before releasing a parcel.
- Directing victims to fake parcel tracking or payment websites.
- Claiming that prohibited or suspicious items have been found inside a parcel.
- Requesting personal information to verify delivery details.
- Using fake parcel tracking numbers or fabricated delivery updates.
- Sending phishing links through SMS messages, emails or messaging applications.
- Creating urgency by claiming that parcels will be returned or destroyed if immediate action is not taken.
- Pretending that identity verification is required before completing delivery.

## Common Warning Signs

- Receiving delivery notifications for parcels that were never ordered.
- Requests for payment before a parcel can be delivered.
- Messages containing suspicious or shortened website links.
- Delivery notifications from unfamiliar courier companies.
- Poor grammar, spelling mistakes or inconsistent branding.
- Unexpected requests for personal or financial information.
- Urgent deadlines requiring immediate payment or verification.
- Parcel tracking websites with unofficial or unfamiliar web addresses.
- Requests for passwords, One-Time Passwords (OTPs) or banking credentials.
- Notifications that do not match any recent online purchases.

## Typical Victim Requests

- Pay customs duties, shipping charges or redelivery fees.
- Click links to track parcels or update delivery information.
- Enter banking or credit card details on fake payment websites.
- Provide personal identification information such as NRIC or passport details.
- Verify addresses or contact information through suspicious websites.
- Download applications for parcel tracking or delivery management.
- Share authentication codes or verification numbers.
- Contact fake customer service representatives through unofficial communication channels.

## Prevention Tips

- Verify parcel notifications by checking your recent purchases and expected deliveries.
- Track parcels only through the courier company's official website or mobile application.
- Avoid clicking links contained in unsolicited delivery messages.
- Confirm unexpected delivery notifications with the sender or retailer.
- Never provide passwords, One-Time Passwords (OTPs) or banking credentials through delivery websites.
- Carefully inspect website addresses before entering payment information.
- Be cautious of requests for unexpected delivery fees.
- Keep devices and web browsers updated with the latest security patches.
- Report suspicious delivery messages instead of responding to them.

## Recommended Actions

- Do not click suspicious links or download attachments.
- Ignore and delete fraudulent parcel notifications.
- Verify parcel information directly with the courier company using official contact channels.
- Contact your bank immediately if payment information has been submitted.
- Preserve SMS messages, emails and screenshots as evidence.
- Report suspicious messages through ScamShield where appropriate.
- File a police report if financial loss or identity theft has occurred.
- Monitor bank accounts and online accounts for suspicious activity.

## Singapore Context

Parcel scams are frequently reported in Singapore due to the widespread use of e-commerce platforms and courier delivery services. Scammers commonly impersonate delivery companies by sending SMS messages or emails claiming that parcels are awaiting delivery, customs clearance or address verification. Victims are then directed to fake websites where they are asked to pay small delivery fees or provide banking credentials. Because many Singapore residents regularly shop online, these scam messages often appear believable.

The Singapore Police Force (SPF) regularly issues advisories warning the public about parcel delivery scams and encourages individuals to verify delivery notifications through official courier websites or mobile applications. ScamShield helps reduce exposure to scam calls and messages, while the Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA) promotes cybersecurity awareness and advises users to avoid clicking suspicious links. Consumers are encouraged to verify delivery requests independently before making any payments or providing personal information.

## Additional Notes

Parcel scams primarily exploit familiarity and routine behaviour. Many people receive genuine delivery notifications regularly, making it easier for scammers to blend fraudulent messages with legitimate communications. The requested payment is often relatively small, which may reduce suspicion and encourage victims to act quickly without verification.

Legitimate courier companies generally provide tracking information through official websites and mobile applications, and any additional delivery charges are communicated using established procedures. Users should independently verify unexpected delivery notifications, especially those requesting urgent payments, confidential information or account credentials. Even if a message contains accurate personal details or convincing branding, it should always be verified before any action is taken.
