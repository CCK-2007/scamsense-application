# E-commerce Scam

## Overview

An e-commerce scam is a type of fraud in which scammers exploit online shopping platforms, social media marketplaces and classified advertisement websites to deceive buyers or sellers into losing money, personal information or valuable goods. These scams often involve fake product listings, fraudulent online stores, counterfeit goods, non-delivery of purchased items or deceptive payment requests. Scammers frequently impersonate legitimate sellers or buyers and take advantage of the trust associated with well-known e-commerce platforms. As online shopping continues to grow, e-commerce scams have become increasingly sophisticated and often rely on convincing product advertisements, attractive prices and time-sensitive offers to persuade victims to act quickly.

## Common Scam Tactics

- Creating fake online stores that imitate legitimate e-commerce websites.
- Advertising popular products at prices significantly lower than the market value.
- Listing products that do not exist or are never intended to be delivered.
- Requesting payment through direct bank transfers or other non-refundable payment methods.
- Sending fake payment confirmations or fraudulent receipts.
- Pretending to be buyers who overpay and request partial refunds.
- Directing users away from official marketplace platforms to continue conversations through private messaging applications.
- Selling counterfeit or poor-quality products while advertising them as genuine.
- Using fake customer reviews, ratings or testimonials to increase credibility.
- Claiming that limited-time promotions or low stock require immediate payment.

## Common Warning Signs

- Prices that appear unusually low compared to similar products.
- Sellers refusing to use secure payment methods provided by the marketplace.
- Requests to continue communication outside the official platform.
- Newly created seller accounts with limited transaction history.
- Poorly designed websites with spelling mistakes or inconsistent branding.
- Requests for full payment before providing shipping information.
- Product images copied from other websites or containing inconsistent descriptions.
- Lack of clear return, refund or contact information.
- Pressure to complete payment immediately to secure a discount or limited stock.
- Payment requests through unfamiliar personal accounts instead of official business accounts.

## Typical Victim Requests

- Transfer money directly to a personal bank account.
- Make payment before receiving the product.
- Pay additional fees for shipping, customs or insurance after the initial purchase.
- Share personal information such as addresses, phone numbers or payment details.
- Click links that lead to fake payment or login pages.
- Continue conversations outside the official marketplace.
- Accept fake proof of payment without verifying the transaction.
- Release goods before confirming that payment has been successfully received.

## Prevention Tips

- Purchase products through reputable and established online marketplaces.
- Verify seller ratings, customer reviews and transaction history before making purchases.
- Compare prices with other sellers to identify unrealistic discounts.
- Use secure payment methods offered by the platform whenever possible.
- Avoid transferring money directly to unknown individuals.
- Keep all communication within the official marketplace messaging system.
- Carefully inspect website URLs before entering payment information.
- Review return, refund and warranty policies before completing a purchase.
- Be cautious of sellers or buyers who create unnecessary urgency.

## Recommended Actions

- Stop communicating with the suspected scammer if suspicious behaviour is detected.
- Do not make additional payments or provide further personal information.
- Report the suspicious seller or buyer through the online marketplace.
- Contact your bank immediately if payment has already been made.
- Preserve screenshots, payment receipts, advertisements and conversations as evidence.
- File a police report if financial loss has occurred.
- Report fraudulent websites or listings to the relevant online platform.
- Monitor bank and credit card transactions for unauthorised activity.

## Singapore Context

E-commerce scams are among the most commonly reported scams in Singapore, particularly through online marketplaces, social media platforms and messaging applications. Victims may encounter fake sellers advertising electronics, concert tickets, luxury goods, household items or rental services at attractive prices. In some cases, scammers also target legitimate sellers by sending fake payment confirmations or impersonating delivery services.

The Singapore Police Force (SPF) regularly issues advisories on emerging e-commerce scam trends and encourages members of the public to verify sellers before making purchases. ScamShield helps users identify scam messages and suspicious communications, while the Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA) promotes safe online practices to reduce the risk of online fraud. Consumers are encouraged to use secure payment methods and remain within trusted marketplace platforms throughout the transaction process.

## Additional Notes

E-commerce scams frequently exploit trust and urgency rather than technical vulnerabilities. Scammers often invest considerable effort into creating convincing advertisements, professional-looking websites and believable customer interactions. A well-designed website or responsive seller does not necessarily indicate legitimacy.

Using secure marketplace features, including buyer protection programmes, verified payment systems and official messaging channels, significantly reduces the likelihood of becoming a victim. Users should independently verify unfamiliar online stores, avoid making decisions based solely on low prices and remember that genuine sellers generally do not discourage the use of secure payment methods or platform protections.
