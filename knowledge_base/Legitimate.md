# Legitimate

## Overview

A legitimate message is a genuine communication sent by a trusted individual, organisation or service provider for valid and non-fraudulent purposes. These messages may include banking notifications, government announcements, appointment reminders, delivery updates, account confirmations, promotional offers from verified businesses or personal conversations. Unlike scam messages, legitimate communications do not attempt to deceive recipients into revealing sensitive information, making unnecessary payments or performing suspicious actions. Although some legitimate messages may contain links, requests for confirmation or account-related information, they generally follow established communication practices and can be verified through official channels.

## Common Characteristics

- Sent by recognised organisations, businesses or known individuals.
- Contains information that matches recent activities or expected communications.
- Uses official contact details, websites or mobile applications.
- Provides clear and accurate information without creating unnecessary panic.
- Includes professional language, consistent branding and appropriate formatting.
- Allows recipients sufficient time to respond without unreasonable pressure.
- Directs users to official customer service channels when assistance is required.
- Requests only information that is necessary for the intended service.
- Provides transparent explanations for any required actions.
- Supports verification through official websites, customer service hotlines or authorised applications.

## Common Indicators of Authenticity

- Communication matches an expected transaction, purchase or service request.
- Website links belong to official domains associated with the organisation.
- Contact information matches details published on official websites.
- Messages contain accurate customer or transaction information.
- Requests follow normal business or government procedures.
- No threats of immediate legal action or account suspension without prior notice.
- No requests for passwords, One-Time Passwords (OTPs) or confidential banking credentials.
- Payment requests are consistent with previously agreed transactions or services.
- Customer support is available through official communication channels.

## Typical User Requests

- Confirm appointments, deliveries or reservations.
- Review account activity or transaction notifications.
- Access official websites or mobile applications through recognised channels.
- Update non-sensitive account information when appropriate.
- Respond to customer service enquiries related to existing services.
- Complete legitimate purchases or service renewals through authorised payment systems.
- Verify identity using official authentication procedures where required.
- Contact customer support for clarification or assistance.

## Prevention Tips

- Continue verifying important communications through official channels when uncertain.
- Access online services by typing official website addresses or using trusted mobile applications instead of relying solely on message links.
- Protect passwords, One-Time Passwords (OTPs) and authentication codes, even when communicating with legitimate organisations.
- Review sender information carefully to identify possible impersonation attempts.
- Keep software and security applications updated to protect against phishing attempts.
- Enable multi-factor authentication for important online accounts.
- Monitor financial accounts regularly for unusual activity.
- Stay informed about common scam techniques that imitate legitimate communications.
- When in doubt, contact the organisation directly using verified contact information.

## Recommended Actions

- Read the message carefully before responding.
- Verify unexpected requests through official communication channels if there is any uncertainty.
- Follow normal procedures for legitimate transactions or account activities.
- Do not disclose confidential information unless you have confirmed the authenticity of the request.
- Contact the organisation directly if any part of the communication appears suspicious.
- Keep records of important communications when appropriate.
- Report suspected impersonation attempts to the affected organisation.
- Continue practising good cybersecurity habits when interacting with online services.

## Singapore Context

Legitimate communications in Singapore are commonly received from banks, government agencies, healthcare providers, educational institutions, delivery companies and other registered organisations. Many organisations provide account notifications, appointment reminders, billing information and service updates through SMS, email, mobile applications or official websites.

Despite being genuine, legitimate messages may resemble scam messages because scammers often imitate trusted organisations. The Singapore Police Force (SPF), ScamShield and the Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA) encourage members of the public to verify unexpected communications before taking action. Financial institutions regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) also implement security measures such as multi-factor authentication and transaction notifications to help customers recognise legitimate account activities and reduce the risk of fraud.

## Additional Notes

Legitimate messages should not be assumed to be safe solely because they appear professional or contain familiar branding. Scammers frequently imitate the appearance of genuine communications, making independent verification an important cybersecurity practice. Users should evaluate messages based on their overall behaviour, including whether they request sensitive information, create unnecessary urgency or direct recipients to unofficial websites.

The absence of common scam indicators does not automatically guarantee authenticity. When uncertainty exists, users should verify communications through official websites, mobile applications or published customer service numbers instead of responding directly to unsolicited messages. Maintaining cautious verification habits helps users distinguish legitimate communications from increasingly sophisticated scam attempts while reducing the risk of becoming a victim of impersonation or phishing attacks.
