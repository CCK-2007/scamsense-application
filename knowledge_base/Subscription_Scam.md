# Subscription Scam

## Overview

A subscription scam is a type of fraud in which scammers deceive individuals into making recurring payments or renewing fake services under the guise of legitimate subscriptions. These scams often involve fraudulent notifications claiming that an existing subscription is about to expire, has been automatically renewed or requires immediate payment to avoid service interruption. In other cases, scammers advertise fake subscription services for streaming platforms, software, digital products or memberships at unusually low prices. Their objective is to obtain payment information, personal data or unauthorised recurring payments by convincing victims that the subscription is genuine or urgently requires action.

## Common Scam Tactics

- Sending fake renewal notices for streaming services, antivirus software or online subscriptions.
- Claiming that a subscription has been automatically renewed and payment has already been processed.
- Creating fake invoices that encourage victims to contact fraudulent customer support.
- Directing victims to phishing websites that imitate legitimate subscription providers.
- Offering heavily discounted subscriptions for popular digital services.
- Requesting payment to prevent account suspension or service interruption.
- Impersonating customer service representatives to obtain payment details.
- Convincing victims to install remote access software to resolve supposed billing issues.
- Using fake receipts or payment confirmations to make the scam appear legitimate.
- Hiding recurring charges within fraudulent subscription offers or misleading free trial promotions.

## Common Warning Signs

- Receiving subscription renewal notices for services that were never purchased.
- Unexpected invoices or receipts for unfamiliar subscriptions.
- Requests for immediate payment to prevent service cancellation.
- Emails or SMS messages containing suspicious links or attachments.
- Customer support phone numbers that are not listed on the company's official website.
- Offers for premium subscriptions at prices that are significantly below market value.
- Requests for passwords, One-Time Passwords (OTPs) or banking credentials.
- Poor grammar, spelling mistakes or inconsistent branding in subscription notifications.
- Pressure to act immediately without allowing time for verification.
- Payment requests through unusual or unofficial methods.

## Typical Victim Requests

- Confirm payment information for a subscription renewal.
- Click links to cancel or renew a subscription.
- Provide credit card or banking details.
- Pay renewal fees or service charges immediately.
- Download software or remote access applications for technical assistance.
- Verify personal information or account credentials.
- Share authentication codes or security verification numbers.
- Purchase discounted subscription packages through unofficial websites.

## Prevention Tips

- Verify subscription notices by logging in directly through the service provider's official website or mobile application.
- Keep track of active subscriptions and recurring payments.
- Avoid clicking links contained in unsolicited renewal notifications.
- Never provide passwords, One-Time Passwords (OTPs) or banking credentials through email or SMS.
- Review bank and credit card statements regularly for unauthorised recurring charges.
- Confirm customer support contact details through official company websites.
- Be cautious of subscription offers that appear significantly cheaper than normal market prices.
- Enable transaction notifications for payment cards where available.
- Cancel subscriptions only through official platforms or verified customer support channels.

## Recommended Actions

- Do not respond to suspicious subscription notifications.
- Verify the subscription directly with the service provider through official contact channels.
- Contact your bank immediately if payment information has been disclosed.
- Cancel compromised payment cards if unauthorised recurring charges are detected.
- Preserve emails, SMS messages, invoices and payment records as evidence.
- Report suspicious messages through ScamShield where appropriate.
- File a police report if financial loss has occurred.
- Monitor bank accounts and credit card statements for further unauthorised transactions.
- Change account passwords if login credentials may have been compromised.

## Singapore Context

Subscription scams are increasingly reported in Singapore as more individuals subscribe to digital services such as streaming platforms, software applications, cloud storage and online memberships. Victims commonly receive fake emails or SMS messages claiming that their subscription has been renewed, payment has failed or their account will be suspended unless immediate action is taken. These messages often direct users to phishing websites that collect payment information or login credentials.

The Singapore Police Force (SPF) regularly advises the public to verify subscription-related communications through official websites and warns against clicking suspicious links contained in unsolicited messages. ScamShield helps reduce exposure to scam calls and messages, while the Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA) promotes cybersecurity awareness and safe online practices to reduce phishing attacks. When subscription scams involve payment cards or financial services, consumers are encouraged to contact their financial institutions immediately to prevent further unauthorised transactions.

## Additional Notes

Subscription scams commonly exploit routine digital services that many people use every day. Because users frequently receive genuine renewal reminders and billing notifications, fraudulent messages can easily blend in with legitimate communications. Scammers often rely on urgency, claiming that accounts will be suspended or automatic payments have already been processed, prompting victims to act without careful verification.

Legitimate service providers generally allow customers to manage subscriptions through official websites or mobile applications and do not request passwords, One-Time Passwords (OTPs) or confidential banking credentials through unsolicited emails or SMS messages. Users should independently verify unexpected subscription notifications, regularly review recurring payments and exercise caution before responding to any communication requesting immediate financial action or sensitive personal information.
