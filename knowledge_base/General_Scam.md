# General Scam

## Overview

A general scam refers to fraudulent activities that do not fall exclusively within a specific scam category such as banking, investment or romance scams. These scams typically rely on deception, impersonation, urgency or false promises to manipulate victims into providing money, personal information or access to their accounts. General scams may occur through SMS messages, emails, phone calls, social media platforms, messaging applications or fake websites. Because scammers constantly adapt their tactics, general scams often combine elements from multiple scam types, making them difficult to classify based on a single theme. The primary objective is to exploit trust, fear or curiosity to convince victims to take actions that benefit the scammer.

## Common Scam Tactics

- Impersonating trusted organisations, companies or service providers.
- Claiming that immediate action is required to avoid penalties or account restrictions.
- Offering fake prizes, rewards, giveaways or promotional benefits.
- Pretending to provide technical support for nonexistent computer or mobile device issues.
- Sending fraudulent links that lead to phishing websites.
- Using spoofed phone numbers, email addresses or social media accounts to appear legitimate.
- Requesting payments for fabricated fees, taxes or administrative charges.
- Creating fake emergencies involving family members or friends to pressure victims into sending money.
- Promising unrealistic financial opportunities or exclusive offers.
- Exploiting current events or trending topics to make fraudulent messages appear more convincing.

## Common Warning Signs

- Unsolicited messages from unknown individuals or organisations.
- Requests for immediate action without allowing time for verification.
- Unexpected offers that seem unusually attractive or too good to be true.
- Requests for confidential personal or financial information.
- Messages containing suspicious or shortened website links.
- Poor grammar, spelling mistakes or inconsistent formatting.
- Pressure to keep the conversation confidential.
- Requests for payment using unconventional or irreversible methods.
- Unexpected phone calls claiming urgent problems that require immediate resolution.
- Inconsistent contact information or unofficial communication channels.

## Typical Victim Requests

- Click a link to verify personal information or account details.
- Transfer money to resolve an alleged issue or claim a reward.
- Share passwords, authentication codes or sensitive personal information.
- Download unfamiliar software or mobile applications.
- Purchase gift cards or vouchers and send the redemption codes.
- Confirm identity using personal documents.
- Continue conversations on private messaging platforms.
- Make repeated payments to resolve fabricated problems or unlock promised benefits.

## Prevention Tips

- Treat unsolicited messages with caution, even if they appear professional.
- Verify unexpected requests using official contact information obtained independently.
- Avoid clicking links or downloading attachments from unknown sources.
- Never share passwords, One-Time Passwords (OTPs) or security codes.
- Take time to evaluate unexpected offers before responding.
- Enable multi-factor authentication on important online accounts.
- Keep devices and security software updated.
- Discuss suspicious requests with trusted family members or friends before taking action.
- Stay informed about common scam techniques and emerging fraud trends.

## Recommended Actions

- Stop communicating with the suspected scammer immediately.
- Do not provide additional personal, financial or authentication information.
- Block the scammer's phone number, email address or online account where appropriate.
- Report suspicious communications through ScamShield if applicable.
- Contact your bank immediately if financial information has been disclosed.
- Preserve messages, screenshots and transaction records as evidence.
- Report the incident to the Singapore Police Force (SPF) if money has been lost or fraud has occurred.
- Monitor financial accounts and online services for suspicious activity.
- Change passwords for affected accounts if login credentials may have been compromised.

## Singapore Context

General scams remain one of the most common forms of fraud reported in Singapore because they cover a wide range of deceptive activities that evolve rapidly. Scammers frequently exploit SMS messages, messaging applications, phone calls, emails and social media platforms to target members of the public. Many scams also impersonate local organisations or exploit public concerns about security, financial matters or government services.

The Singapore Police Force (SPF) regularly publishes scam alerts and educational materials to help the public recognise new scam variants. ScamShield assists users by filtering scam calls and messages, while the Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA) promotes cybersecurity awareness and safe online behaviour. Depending on the nature of the scam, other government agencies may also issue public advisories relating to specific fraud trends.

## Additional Notes

General scams are highly adaptable and frequently evolve to exploit changing technologies, public events and consumer behaviour. Although the specific stories used by scammers may differ, many scams rely on similar psychological techniques, including creating urgency, establishing false authority, promising unrealistic rewards or exploiting fear of financial loss.

Users should evaluate unexpected communications based on the behaviours they encourage rather than the specific claims they make. Requests involving secrecy, immediate payment, disclosure of sensitive information or unusual financial transactions should always be treated with caution. Developing good verification habits and remaining sceptical of unsolicited requests significantly reduces the likelihood of becoming a scam victim.
